About This System

What Is This System?

The AI-Powered Adaptive Critical Reading Learning System (ACRLS) is a web-based educational technology prototype developed as part of a doctoral research project in English Education. The system is designed to support undergraduate students in developing higher-order critical reading skills through adaptive content delivery and AI-assisted diagnostic feedback.

Unlike conventional e-learning platforms, ACRLS dynamically adjusts reading materials, question complexity, and feedback depth based on individual learner performance — operationalizing the principles of adaptive learning theory within a culturally responsive pedagogical framework.

Research Context

This system serves as the primary research instrument for a doctoral dissertation examining the effectiveness of AI-supported adaptive learning in improving critical reading competencies among undergraduate students at IAIN Sultan Amai Gorontalo.

The research employs a mixed-methods design, collecting both quantitative data (reading accuracy scores, response times, level progression) and qualitative data (reflective responses, cultural interpretations, and ideological analysis) to produce a comprehensive understanding of AI-mediated critical literacy development.

Learning Objectives

Grounded in Bloom's Revised Taxonomy (Anderson & Krathwohl, 2001), this system targets the higher-order cognitive domains of analysis, evaluation, and creation within the context of critical reading.
